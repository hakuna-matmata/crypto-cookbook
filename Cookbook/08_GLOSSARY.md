## A

**Address**
A unique identifier for sending/receiving crypto. Like an email address but for blockchain. Each network has different address formats.

**Airdrop**
Free tokens distributed to wallet addresses, often as marketing or rewards for early users.

**AMM (Automated Market Maker)**
A smart contract that enables trading by holding reserves of tokens. Users trade against the pool rather than other users. Examples: Uniswap, Orca.

**APY (Annual Percentage Yield)**
The yearly return on an investment, including compound interest. A 10% APY means $100 becomes $110 after one year.

**Arbitrage**
Profiting from price differences between markets. Buy low on one exchange, sell high on another.

**Attribute (NFT)**
A trait or characteristic of an NFT (e.g., "Blue Background", "Diamond Eyes"). Attributes determine rarity.

---

## B

**Bid-Ask Spread**
The difference between the highest buy price (bid) and lowest sell price (ask). Tighter spreads = more liquid market.

**Block**
A bundle of transactions added to the blockchain together. Each block links to the previous one, forming a chain.

**Block Time**
How long between blocks. Solana: ~400ms. Ethereum: ~12 seconds. Bitcoin: ~10 minutes.

**Blockchain**
A distributed database where transactions are grouped into blocks and linked together cryptographically. Cannot be altered after recording.

**Brier Score**
A measure of prediction accuracy. Lower is better. 0 = perfect prediction. Used to evaluate prediction markets.

**Bridge**
A protocol that moves tokens between different blockchains. Example: Moving USDC from Ethereum to Solana.

---

## C

**CEX (Centralized Exchange)**
A company that operates a cryptocurrency exchange. Examples: Coinbase, Binance, Kraken. They hold your keys.

**cNFT (Compressed NFT)**
NFTs stored using merkle tree compression. Much cheaper to mint but requires off-chain indexing.

**Collateral**
Assets deposited as security for a loan or leveraged position. If you can't repay, collateral is seized.

**Concentrated Liquidity**
Providing liquidity only within a specific price range. Higher capital efficiency but requires active management.

**Confirmation**
A block added after your transaction's block. More confirmations = higher certainty transaction is final.

**Consensus**
How blockchain nodes agree on the state of the ledger. Methods include Proof of Work, Proof of Stake, Proof of History.

**Cross-Chain**
Operations or protocols that work across multiple blockchains.

**Custodial**
When a third party holds your private keys. Exchanges are custodial. "Not your keys, not your coins."

---

## D

**DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization)**
An organization governed by smart contracts and token holder votes rather than traditional management.

**dApp (Decentralized Application)**
An application running on blockchain smart contracts rather than traditional servers.

**DeFi (Decentralized Finance)**
Financial services built on blockchain without traditional intermediaries. Includes lending, trading, staking.

**Delegation**
Assigning your staked tokens to a validator. You keep ownership but they earn rewards on your behalf.

**Derivative**
A financial contract whose value depends on something else (underlying asset, event outcome, etc.).

**DEX (Decentralized Exchange)**
An exchange operating via smart contracts without a central company. Examples: Jupiter, Uniswap, Orca.

---

## E

**Epoch**
A defined time period on Solana (~2-3 days). Staking rewards and validator changes happen at epoch boundaries.

**ERC-721**
Ethereum's NFT standard. Defines how non-fungible tokens work on Ethereum.

---

## F

**Finality**
The point where a transaction becomes irreversible. Different blockchains have different finality times.

**Floor Price**
The lowest listed price for any NFT in a collection. Baseline value for the collection.

**Front-running**
Executing a transaction ahead of another known transaction to profit. A form of MEV extraction.

**Fungible**
Interchangeable. One SOL equals any other SOL. Opposite of non-fungible (unique).

**Funding Rate**
Payments between long and short positions in perpetual futures. Keeps perp prices aligned with spot prices.

---

## G

**Gas**
Transaction fees paid to process blockchain transactions. On Solana, fees are extremely low (~$0.0001).

**Governance**
The system for making decisions about a protocol. Usually involves token-weighted voting.

**Governance Token**
A token that grants voting rights on protocol decisions. Examples: JUP, UNI.

---

## H

**Haircut**
Discount applied to collateral value. A 20% haircut means $100 of SOL counts as $80 collateral.

**Hardware Wallet**
Physical device that stores private keys offline. Examples: Ledger, Trezor. Most secure storage method.

**Hash**
A fixed-length string generated from input data. Same input always produces same hash. Used to verify data integrity.

**Hedge**
A position taken to reduce risk of another position. Insurance, not profit-seeking.

**Hot Wallet**
A wallet connected to the internet. Convenient but less secure than cold storage.

---

## I

**IL (Impermanent Loss)**
Loss experienced by liquidity providers when token prices change. Called "impermanent" because it reverses if prices return to original levels.

**Initial Margin (IM)**
The collateral required to open a leveraged position.

**IPFS (InterPlanetary File System)**
Decentralized storage network. NFT images are often stored on IPFS.

---

## J

**Jupiter**
Solana's primary DEX aggregator. Finds best swap prices across all liquidity sources.

---

## K

**KYC (Know Your Customer)**
Identity verification required by regulated exchanges. Includes ID checks and address verification.

---

## L

**Leverage**
Using borrowed funds to amplify position size. 5x leverage = control 5x your capital. Amplifies both gains and losses.

**Limit Order**
An order to buy/sell only at a specific price or better. May not execute if price never reaches limit.

**Liquidation**
Forced closure of a position when collateral falls below required level. Happens automatically in DeFi.

**Liquidity**
How easily an asset can be bought/sold without moving the price. High liquidity = tight spreads, easy trading.

**LP (Liquidity Provider)**
Someone who deposits tokens into a liquidity pool to enable trading. Earns trading fees.

**LST (Liquid Staking Token)**
A token representing staked assets. Examples: mSOL, JitoSOL. Earns staking rewards while remaining liquid.

---

## M

**Maintenance Margin (MM)**
Minimum collateral required to keep a leveraged position open. Fall below this = liquidation.

**Market Order**
An order to buy/sell immediately at current market price. Guaranteed execution, variable price.

**MEV (Maximal Extractable Value)**
Profit extracted by manipulating transaction ordering. Includes front-running and sandwich attacks.

**Mempool**
Waiting area for unconfirmed transactions. Transactions visible here before being included in blocks.

**Merkle Tree**
Data structure used for efficient verification of large data sets. Used in compressed NFTs.

**Metadata**
Data describing an NFT (name, image, attributes). Usually stored off-chain with link on-chain.

**Metaplex**
Solana's NFT infrastructure. Handles minting, metadata, royalties, and collections.

**Mint**
Create a new token or NFT. The first issuance of an asset on blockchain.

**mSOL**
Marinade Finance's liquid staking token for SOL.

---

## N

**NFT (Non-Fungible Token)**
A unique digital asset with verifiable ownership. Each NFT is distinct, unlike fungible tokens.

**Node**
A computer running blockchain software. Maintains a copy of the ledger and validates transactions.

**Non-Custodial**
You control your own private keys. No third party can access your funds.

---

## O

**On-Chain**
Data or actions recorded directly on the blockchain.

**Off-Chain**
Data or actions that happen outside the blockchain.

**Open Interest (OI)**
Total value of outstanding derivative positions. High OI = many active positions.

**Oracle**
A service that provides external data to smart contracts. Example: Price feeds for liquidations.

**Order Book**
List of buy and sell orders at various prices. Traditional exchange structure.

---

## P

**Perp (Perpetual Future)**
A derivative that tracks an asset's price with no expiration date. Funding rates keep price aligned with spot.

**pNFT (Programmable NFT)**
NFT with on-chain enforced royalties and transfer rules.

**PoH (Proof of History)**
Solana's consensus mechanism. Cryptographic timestamps enable faster processing.

**PoS (Proof of Stake)**
Consensus mechanism where validators stake tokens as collateral. Used by Ethereum, Solana (combined with PoH).

**PoW (Proof of Work)**
Consensus mechanism where miners solve puzzles to create blocks. Used by Bitcoin.

**Position**
Your exposure to an asset or market. Long position profits when price rises. Short position profits when price falls.

**Price Impact**
How much your trade moves the market price. Large trades have more impact.

**Private Key**
Secret number that proves ownership and signs transactions. Never share this.

**Protocol**
A set of rules or smart contracts defining how a system works. Example: Drift Protocol, Marinade Protocol.

**Public Key**
Your wallet address, derived from private key. Safe to share. Where people send you crypto.

---

## Q

**Quadratic Voting**
Voting system where cost increases quadratically. Reduces whale dominance.

---

## R

**Rarity**
How uncommon an NFT's traits are. Rare traits often = higher value.

**Rent**
On Solana, small deposit required to hold accounts open. Refunded when account closed.

**Revoke**
Cancel a previously granted approval. Important for security hygiene.

**RPC (Remote Procedure Call)**
The connection point between your wallet and the blockchain network.

**Royalty**
Fee paid to NFT creator on secondary sales. Usually 5-10%.

**Rug Pull**
Scam where project creators abandon after taking money.

---

## S

**Sandwich Attack**
MEV strategy: attacker front-runs and back-runs your transaction to extract profit.

**Seed Phrase**
12-24 words that can recover your wallet. Master backup. Never share or store digitally.

**Self-Custody**
Controlling your own private keys. Full ownership and responsibility.

**Slashing**
Penalty for validator misbehavior. Part of staked tokens destroyed.

**Slippage**
Difference between expected and actual execution price. Set slippage tolerance to control maximum acceptable difference.

**Smart Contract**
Code that executes automatically on blockchain when conditions are met.

**Spot Trading**
Buying/selling actual tokens for immediate delivery. No derivatives or leverage.

**SPL Token**
Solana's token standard. Defines how tokens work on Solana.

**Stablecoin**
Cryptocurrency designed to maintain stable value, usually $1. Examples: USDC, USDT.

**Stake/Staking**
Locking tokens to secure the network and earn rewards.

**Stop Loss**
Order that automatically sells if price falls to specified level. Limits losses.

---

## T

**Token**
Any digital asset on a blockchain. Includes cryptocurrencies, governance tokens, NFTs.

**TPS (Transactions Per Second)**
Measure of blockchain throughput. Solana: 65,000+ TPS. Ethereum: 15-30 TPS.

**Transaction**
A signed request to change blockchain state. Examples: transfers, swaps, smart contract calls.

**TVL (Total Value Locked)**
Total assets deposited in a DeFi protocol. Measure of protocol size.

---

## U

**UMA**
Oracle protocol used by Polymarket. Uses optimistic approach with dispute mechanism.

**Unified Margin**
System where one collateral pool backs multiple positions. More capital efficient.

**Unstaking**
Withdrawing previously staked tokens. May have delay (epoch boundary on Solana).

---

## V

**Validator**
Node that participates in creating new blocks. Earns rewards for honest operation.

**Volatility**
How much price fluctuates. High volatility = large price swings.

---

## W

**Wallet**
Software that stores private keys and enables blockchain interaction. Doesn't actually "hold" cryptoâ€”holds keys that prove ownership.

**Wash Trading**
Buying/selling with yourself to fake volume. Form of market manipulation.

**Whale**
Large holder who can move markets with their trades.

**Whitelist**
Approved addresses for early/exclusive access to mints or features.

---

## X-Y-Z

**Yield**
Return earned on an investment. Can come from staking, lending, liquidity provision.

**Yield Farming**
Moving assets between protocols to maximize returns. Often involves multiple layers of yield.

**Zero-Knowledge Proof**
Cryptographic method to prove something without revealing the underlying information.
